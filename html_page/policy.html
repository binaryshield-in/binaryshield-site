Privacy Policy – Binary Shield

Last Updated: September 2025

At Binary Shield, your privacy is our priority. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal information when you access our website, services, or open-source projects.

1. Information We Collect

We may collect the following types of information:

  • Personal Information – Name, email address, contact details (when you register, contact us, or subscribe).
  • Technical Data – IP address, browser type, device information, and usage patterns.
  • Service Data – Information related to penetration testing, vulnerability assessments, or OSINT tools when used responsibly.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information collected to:

  • Provide and improve our cybersecurity services.
  • Respond to inquiries and support requests.
  • Analyze traffic and usage for better user experience.
  • Ensure compliance with security and legal obligations.
  • Share updates about new tools, services, or security research (only if subscribed).

3. Data Protection & Security

  • We implement strict technical, administrative, and organizational controls to protect your data from unauthorized access or misuse.
  • Open-source contributions do not include personal data unless explicitly provided by the contributor.

4. Sharing of Information

  • We do not sell or rent your personal data to third parties.
  • Information may be shared only with trusted partners or service providers required to deliver our services.
  • We may disclose data if required by law, regulation, or to protect our legal rights.

5. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

  • Our website may use cookies for analytics and performance optimization.
  • You can manage or disable cookies through your browser settings.

6. Your Rights

As a user, you have the right to:

  • Access, update, or delete your personal data.
  • Opt out of marketing or newsletters at any time.
  • Request details of data collected about you.

7. Third-Party Links

Our website may include links to third-party platforms (e.g., GitHub, LinkedIn, YouTube). We are not responsible for the privacy practices of those platforms.

8. Policy Updates

We may revise this Privacy Policy periodically. Any changes will be updated on this page with a revised "Last Updated" date.
